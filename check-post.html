From myths on COVID treatment to vaccine: How are fact-checkers countering the misinfodemic?
COVID-19 took us all by surprise and the past few months have been nothing less than chaotic. Amid all the chaos, timely fact-checks helped us cut through the noise and declutter social media. As the harms of misinformation range from minor to life threatening, attempts to counter misinformation are needed more than ever.
Meet Kritika Goel, Assistant Editor, the Quint
Kritika Goel is Assistant Editor of Webqoof, an independent fact-checking initiative of The Quint. A journalist by training and a passionate fact-checker, Kritika talks to Meedan about her journey into the world of fact-checking and her vision for the future.
    As a journalist we are taught to ask questions. For me an important question was - what is the source of the information? Most of the time, there was no answer. That is when I shifted to full-time fact-checking. An important strategy for me is to do more ground reports. This gives us stronger visual evidence.

I wish we have days where we look for misinformation and do not find them. Even if I can't wish for a world without misinformation, I definitely hope that people verify information before they share it.

Meedan is a global technology not-for-profit that builds open-source tools for newsrooms to fact-check their reporting, and for audiences to fact-check what they read online.
